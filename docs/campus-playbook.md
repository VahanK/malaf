# Malaf — Campus Bubble Playbook (September weapon, prepped in August)

**Principle:** a bubble = saturation of ONE closed network, not reach across many. Facebook's campus-by-campus rule: don't touch campus #2 until campus #1 says "everyone's on it."
**Timing:** Lebanese unis are empty until late Sept. NOW = working freelancers (the 30-day gate). AUGUST = prep this kit. SEPTEMBER = ignite.

## 1. Target selection
- One campus, one faculty first. Ranked by freelancer density: ALBA (design) · LAU Comm Arts · AUB Architecture/FEA · USEK/NDU audiovisual · BAU design.
- Deciding criterion: where do YOU have real friends inside? Access beats demographics.
- Bubble-achieved metric (write before starting): **100 cards in one faculty within 3 weeks** → then campus #2.

## 2. Stickers (the physical layer)
- **Placement hierarchy:** laptop lids (peer-applied, permanent, high-status) > water bottles > bathroom stalls/elevators (high dwell, gets cleaned — part of the game) > notice boards (last resort).
- **Designs (3):**
  1. Gold ملف mark + malaf.work — pure identity, must be aesthetic enough to *want* on a laptop
  2. "مين بيحكي عن المصاري؟ مش أنا" + QR
  3. "I have a card. Do you?" + QR
- **Rule:** stickers are distributed through users (founder packs), never wheat-pasted by "the brand." Peer stickers spread; corporate stickers get scraped.
- Cost: ~500 die-cut ≈ $30–60 at Beirut print shops. QR goes to a campus-coded landing (utm per campus → the counter page).

## 3. Campus founders (the ignition — people, not posters)
- 5–10 per faculty: THE event photographer, THE cv-designer, THE makeup artist of the dorm. The people already being paid by other students.
- Their deal: lifetime founder pricing + a **real printed premium card** (NFC if cheap, ~$2–5/card) + featured on the IG wall + sticker pack to hand out.
- Their job: exist loudly. Card at parties, QR in bio, "just send my malaf" in group chats.

## 4. Moment hijacks (attach to events that already exist)
- **End-of-semester exhibitions:** student work displayed → "Like it? Scan to hire them" QR next to each piece. The gallery becomes a hiring surface.
- **Club workshop (highest single-leverage move):** "How to get paid as a student freelancer" for the photography/design/entrepreneurship club — pricing, deposits, chasing politely — ends with the room building their cards live. 30 signups with activation done in-session. Clubs need content; you need rooms. Everyone wins.
- Job fairs, talent shows, open days: same pattern — be the QR at the place where student work meets an audience.

## 5. The status game (digital bubble mechanics)
- **Handle scarcity:** malaf.work/rami goes to the first Rami. Announce it plainly: short handles run out. Converts "later" into "now."
- **The counter page:** "ALBA 73 · LAU 41 · AUB 38" — inter-uni rivalry is free marketing energy in Lebanon. One page, auto-counting, screenshot-bait.
- **The featured wall:** IG account reposting the best student card daily. Being featured = micro-fame inside the bubble. Founders first.
- **Geo-fenced ads (echo, not ignition):** $3–5/day IG/TikTok on campus coordinates showing THAT campus's cards — reads as campus gossip, not advertising. Only after the physical layer is visible.

## 6. Economics honesty
- Students mostly ride free tier. Expected paid conversion on campus: low single digits — fine. Campus value = farm volume, culture, handle land-grab, and the graduating pipeline (this year's design student is next year's paying freelancer).
- Paying customers remain working freelancers — the founder-deal track runs in parallel and OUTRANKS campus work until the 30-day gate passes.
- Budget for one campus bubble: stickers $50 + printed founder cards $40 + geo ads $50 + workshop $0 = **~$150 per campus.**
