# Supply-side analysis — "Wolf of Bey" Discord, #dropservicing-students

**Source:** full channel export, 5,027 non-bot messages from 459 authors, Dec 2022 → Jul 2026 (raw CSV in `research/`, gitignored).
**What this channel is:** the course-student community (drop-servicing course, Teachable-based — students resell freelance services). Activity peaked in 2023 (4,070 msgs) and has tailed to ~100-150/yr since — so treat this as a *historical* picture of the aspiring-seller pipeline, not a live market. Top 10 authors produce 36% of messages (instructor-heavy).

## Who these people are

Young Lebanese (many students) learning to sell services — mostly **websites/Shopify builds** (402 mentions, by far #1), then design (48), video editing (26), ads (19), SEO (17), SMM (16). They are the *seller pipeline* Malaf's campus playbook targets: they already want to sell services and are being taught to do it with zero infrastructure.

## Their pains, ranked (1,020 of 5,027 messages are questions)

1. **Finding clients / outreach — 131 msgs.** Cold DMs, niche selection, "how many businesses have you reached out to." Malaf doesn't solve demand-gen directly; the card is the *landing surface* for whatever outreach they do.
2. **Getting paid from Lebanon — 120 msgs.** Constant gateway anxiety: "I need a payment gateway based in Lebanon," fresh-account workarounds. Local rails dominate the actual answers: **Whish/OMT mentioned 47×, Binance/USDT 24×, vs PayPal 10× and Payoneer 8×** — strong confirmation that payment-rails-as-display-config (not integrations) matches reality.
   - **Competitive intel from the chat:** "Whish, areeba have a pay by link platform and monty too" — Whish, Areeba, and Monty all have pay-by-link products. Payments-side players are one step from freelancer front-office territory; track them in the competitor scan.
3. **Credibility / looking professional — 73 msgs.** The purest Malaf evidence in all the data:
   - "how am i supposed to look credible to my prospect when i have literally 0 followers on instagram"
   - another literally asks whether to **buy fake followers** to look credible
   - recommended remedy inside the community is exactly Malaf's block list: "portfolio, reviews/testimonials, why choose us, before/after"
   The felt need behind Malaf ("look professional without followers or a website") is voiced verbatim, unprompted.
4. **Pricing / what to charge — 24 msgs.** Beginners freeze when clients ask for prices. Malaf's services-with-units schema forces that decision once, publicly.
5. **Fulfillment — 6 msgs** but one is strategy gold: "fulfill through Lebanese freelancers which will make your life 10 times easier" — Lebanese freelancer supply is treated as an arbitrage asset; those fulfilling freelancers are also Malaf targets.

## Platform reality

**Instagram is the storefront (177 mentions)** — profiles + reels are how sellers present and prospect; WhatsApp (37) is where deals go; marketplace mentions are marginal (Upwork 65 mostly as a *sourcing* tool for fulfillment, Fiverr just 5). Nobody mentions link-in-bio tools or portfolio builders — the gap between "Instagram profile" and "real website" is unoccupied in this community's mind. That's Malaf's slot.

## Implications for Malaf

- **Campus playbook targeting is right:** this pipeline of aspiring sellers exists, skews to web/Shopify + design, and churns through cohorts. The 2023 peak also warns: course-driven communities decay — recruit through the *course/community owners* ("Jad"/Wolf of Bey himself is a distribution channel candidate).
- **Pitch language, verbatim from the market:** "look credible to your prospect without followers" beats any feature list.
- **Demo slice should show**: testimonials, before/after, portfolio, clear prices — the exact credibility artifacts the community prescribes to each other.
- **Watch Whish/Areeba/Monty pay-by-link** as adjacent movers into freelancer tooling.

## Caveats

Peak activity was 2023; current channel is quiet — pains may have shifted with AI tooling (only 5 automation/AI-services mentions, surprisingly low, likely because the channel predates the AI-services wave). Regex counting, same ±10% caveat as the demand analysis. Two more channel exports are incoming; a combined synthesis should follow once all four are in.
